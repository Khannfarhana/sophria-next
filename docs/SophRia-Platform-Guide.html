01 · Overview

What the platform is

A single system that connects three groups of people — your customers, your chauffeurs, and your dispatch team — around one job: booking a luxury airport transfer, taking payment safely, and getting a driver to the door on time.

Customers
Book & ride

Choose a trip and vehicle, get an instant quote, pay securely, track the booking, and hand the driver a one‑time code to start the ride.

Chauffeurs
Drive & earn

Apply with documents, get approved by you, accept assigned rides, start each ride with the passenger's code, complete it, and get paid automatically.

You & dispatch
Run the business

Confirm bookings, assign drivers, adjust fares, review chauffeur applications, and manage your entire rate card from one screen.

The platform sits between the passenger and the chauffeur. It quotes every airport fare from the official Toronto Pearson (GTAA) tariff, holds the money on the customer's card when they book, dispatches a driver, and charges the card once the ride is complete. Roughly 90% of the business is Pearson airport transfers, and the whole system is tuned around that.

03 · Using the app

For your customers

The booking flow is six clear steps. A running price is shown the whole way, and the final fare is always recalculated on our servers — the customer can never alter what they're charged.

  1. Choose the tripAirport, one‑way, or by the hour. Enter pickup and drop‑off — with up to five stops along the way. The map draws the exact route.
  2. Pick date & timeThe picker only allows future times; a past time is rejected before they can continue.
  3. Select a vehicleEach vehicle shows its own live price for this trip. The SUV leads the list, per your direction.
  4. Passenger detailsName, phone, number of passengers, and bags. If a party is larger than the vehicle seats, the app says so.
  5. Review & confirmA full breakdown — fare, airport fee, HST, tip — before they commit. They receive a booking reference (e.g. SR‑A1B2C3) and a 4‑digit start code.
  6. Pay when confirmedOnce your dispatch confirms, the customer gets a secure Stripe payment link. Paying holds the amount on their card.

After booking, the customer can

Track the ride's status live, see their driver once assigned, hand the driver their 4‑digit code to start the ride, view the full fare breakdown, and cancel — with the refund shown up front before they confirm the cancellation.

The start code

The passenger reads a 4‑digit code on their screen and gives it to the chauffeur at pickup. The ride can't start without it — proof the right passenger is actually there. Drivers can't see the code themselves.

03 · Using the app

For your chauffeurs

Chauffeurs are your own fleet and partner drivers who bring their own vehicles. Everyone applies, you approve, and only then can they drive.

Getting approved

  1. ApplyFull Ontario G licence, 3+ years' experience, and vehicle details — class, make, model, year, and limo plate.
  2. Upload documentsEleven required items: driver's licence, vehicle‑for‑hire licence, commercial insurance, vehicle safety & ownership, right to work, background‑check consent, and four photos of the vehicle with the plate visible.
  3. Accept the chauffeur termsRecorded with a timestamp and version, so there's a clear record of agreement.
  4. Wait for your reviewYou see the full application and every document, and approve or decline. Approval grants driver access within about a minute.

Working a ride

  1. Accept the assignmentWhen you assign them a ride, they see it in their portal and accept (or decline, which returns it to the pool).
  2. Start with the codeAt pickup, they enter the passenger's 4‑digit code. The ride can only start close to the scheduled time.
  3. Complete the rideOne tap marks it done. This is what triggers payment capture and their payout.
  4. Get paidTheir share (default 75% of the fare) plus 100% of any tip is credited automatically. Earnings are tracked in their portal.

An overcharge we corrected

The GTAA tariff already includes tax — its own rate card says so. The system had been adding your markup and 13% HST on top of a number that already contained tax, so customers were being overcharged by ~11% on airport trips. That's now fixed: tax is applied once. Prices came down and now sit in line with the market.

05 · Payments

Hold now, charge after the ride

When a customer pays, the amount is held on their card — secured but not taken. It's captured only when the ride is completed. Money is never charged for a ride that hasn't happened.

Held

Customer paid; the amount is reserved on their card. The booking is secured and a driver can be assigned.

Charged

The ride completed. The held amount is captured and the chauffeur is paid.

Released / refunded

Cancelled. The hold is released, or a refund is issued against the cancellation policy.

One real limit, handled honestly

A card hold only lasts about a week. For bookings far in the future — weddings, proms booked months ahead — the card is charged up front instead, and the checkout screen says which is happening. If a hold ever lapses before a ride, you're emailed so it can be settled by hand; the customer is never quietly given a free ride.

The system also protects against the awkward edges automatically: if a fare is changed, any old unpaid payment link is voided so the customer can't pay the previous, lower amount; and if a ride's payment can't be captured, you're alerted rather than the driver being told their completed ride didn't complete.

06 · Cancellations

Cancellations & refunds

The refund depends on how close to pickup the customer cancels. The tip is always returned in full, and the penalty is shown before they confirm.

When they cancelFee keptRefunded
More than 12 hours before0%100%
12 to 6 hours before25%75%
6 hours to 15 minutes before50%50%
15 minutes to pickup75%25%
At or after pickup (no‑show)100%0%

On a held booking, a cancellation fee is taken straight from the hold and the rest released instantly — the customer never waits days for a refund. When money was already charged, the balance is refunded.

Needs your decision

This ladder follows your 14 July instruction. It differs from an earlier policy you approved on 6 July (full refund beyond 24h, 50% at 12–24h, nothing inside 12h). At 8 hours before pickup, one refunds half and the other refunds nothing. We've used the newer one — please confirm which you want, and we'll lock it in.

07 · Your tools

Managing your prices

Your entire rate card — 136 airport tariffs and every rate, fee, markup, and split — lives in one screen. You change a price, see exactly what it does, and publish.

  1. Change a rateEdit any figure: markup, airport fee, meet‑&‑greet, HST, the driver's share, per‑km rates, surcharges.
  2. See the effect instantlyA live preview shows what your change does to real fares — e.g. "Terminal 3 → Downtown: $139.95 → $126.10" — and what you'd keep per ride, before you commit.
  3. Give a reasonRequired, and kept forever against that version — so you always know who changed what, and why.
  4. PublishNew bookings quote the new prices immediately. Your public pricing and terms pages update to match automatically.
Safe by design

Publishing never overwrites — it saves a new version, so you can always look back or roll back. Impossible values are refused (a mistyped HST of 1300% can't be saved). And already‑booked rides keep the price they were quoted; changing a rate today never rewrites what someone paid last week.

08 · Economics

What you actually keep per ride

The rate screen shows your true margin next to every fare — after the driver's share, the taxes and fees you pass on, and the card processing cost that was previously invisible.

Customer pays$90.99
HST — remitted to the CRA$10.47
Airport fee — remitted to the GTAA$17.25
= your fare base$63.27
Chauffeur's share (75%)$47.45
= gross$15.82
Card processing (~2.9% + 30¢)$2.94
Your net, per ride$12.88

The HST and airport fee aren't your money — you collect and pass them on. The card fee takes about 18% of your gross and had never been accounted for anywhere. Seeing this net figure while you set prices means every pricing decision is made on what you truly keep, not on the headline fare.

09 · Trust

Security & reliability

The platform is built so that customers see only their own information, prices can't be tampered with, and payments are handled by trusted providers.

Payments
Handled by Stripe

Card details never touch our servers. Every payment event is verified as genuinely from Stripe before it's acted on.

Fares
Server‑calculated

The price is always recomputed on our side. A customer's browser can show a preview but can never change what they're charged.

Access
Locked to the owner

Customers see only their own bookings. Chauffeurs see only rides assigned to them. Only approved admins can confirm, assign, or reprice.

Ride start
Protected by a code

A ride can only begin with the passenger's one‑time code — which drivers themselves cannot read — so rides start with the right person present.

Access to every piece of data is enforced at the database itself, not just in the app — so the protections hold even against direct attempts to reach the data. Chauffeur documents are stored privately with size and file‑type limits, and every price change is recorded with who made it and why.

How the pieces fit

The fare engine (lib/pricing, lib/tariff) reads a live, versioned rate card and produces every quote; the same code runs on the server for the authoritative fare. Payments (lib/payments) manage the Stripe hold‑and‑capture lifecycle. Bookings and driver actions (lib/actions) are server‑side and role‑gated. Notifications (lib/mailer) are fire‑and‑forget so email never blocks an action.

Operational notes

Database changes are applied as ordered migration files. A dedicated staging environment is recommended before further production changes. A full engineering‑level design document (data model, database functions, security policies) is maintained separately in the repository.
